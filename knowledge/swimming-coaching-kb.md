## 1. Energy systems for the 50m freestyle

The 50m free is **~65–75% anaerobic**, and at race intensity the aerobic contribution is as little as **~4%** — the swimmer essentially does not need oxygen to finish the race [source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sports-and-active-living/articles/10.3389/fspor.2025.1751687/full] [source: https://www.yourswimlog.com/sprint-freestyle-breathing/]. Two anaerobic systems matter:

| System | Fuel | Dominates | Train with | Rest |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Alactic (ATP-PCr)** | Stored ATP + creatine phosphate | Efforts <10s (a flat-out 25m) | Short max sprints, full quality | **90s–7min** (full recovery) [source: https://swimswam.com/improve-sprint-freestyle-speed/] |
| **Anaerobic glycolytic (lactate)** | Glycogen → lactate | ~15–40s efforts (the 50 itself) | Near-max 50s, short-rest 25s | 1:30–3:00 (production) or 20s (tolerance) [source: https://oswestryotters.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/Lactate-Production.pdf] |
| **Aerobic** | Oxygen | Recovery, work capacity between reps | Threshold/technique volume | 15–40s |

**Key rule:** for neuromuscular *speed* work, rest is not optional — short rest converts a speed set into an endurance set and blunts the adaptation [source: https://www.yourswimlog.com/best-sets-for-50-freestyle/]. This is also Julian's quad-protective variable (cramps did not recur at ≥2 min rest).

## 2. Sprint freestyle technique

### Stroke rate vs distance-per-stroke (DPS) — the central tradeoff
- Elite 50m freestylers use a stroke rate of **55–65 cycles/min** (Paris 2024 men's finalists averaged **62.2**) [source: https://swimswam.com/stroke-rate-for-sprint-freestyle/].
- **For the 50m, stroke *length* (DPS) is the stronger predictor of speed**; stroke-rate correlations are trivial or slightly negative (men ρ=0.57 for SL vs trivial for SR) [source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12541611/]. At the top, everyone has a high stroke rate — the differentiator is who *holds water and maintains DPS under fatigue*.
- Velocity = DPS × stroke rate. Raising DPS ~0.1 m/stroke (~10%) yields a meaningful speed gain at constant rate [source: https://blog.tritonwear.com/impact-of-stroke-rate-dps-on-freestyle-speed].
- **Coaching implication for Julian:** chase DPS (fewer strokes per length at speed) *without* dropping rate — not just thrashing faster. His sprint target of 7 strokes/length is the DPS lever.

### SWOLF
- SWOLF = (length time in seconds) + (strokes for that length). Lower = more efficient. Two levers: swim faster, or take fewer strokes [source: https://www.yourswimlog.com/swolf-swimming/] [source: https://swimswam.com/swolf-for-swimming/].
- It is a **personal** metric (varies with height/wingspan) — track change over time, don't compare to others.
- A good SWOLF is *sustainable and repeatable with good technique*, not just low — don't game it by gliding.

### Breathing for the 50m
- Elite sprinters take **0–2 breaths** in a 50m. A single breath in a 25m sprint slowed swimmers by **2.8%** [source: https://www.yourswimlog.com/sprint-freestyle-breathing/].
- The mid-race urge to breathe is a **CO2 alarm, not an O2 alarm**. Sprint performance gains come from *lowering the urgency to breathe*, i.e. CO2 tolerance.
- Train CO2 tolerance with low/no-breath short reps (15–50m) and hypoxic ladders (e.g. 4×50 breathing 3/5/7), **1–2×/week max** to avoid overtraining [source: https://blog.arenaswim.com/en/training-technique/learn-tohold-breath/]. Julian's "no breath first 10m then every 2" sprints and "every-5 continuous" cool-down align with this.

## 3. Push-offs, underwaters and breakouts (the biggest free seconds)

- The push-off is the **fastest point of the race** — the underwater goal is to hold that velocity as long as legally useful (15m limit) [source: https://athletesuntapped.com/blog/the-fifth-stroke-mastering-underwater-phase-optimization-in-swimming/].
- **Streamline:** hands clasped, arms squeezing the ears, body long; power from the torso, not the knees.
- **Dolphin kick:** small, fast amplitude from the hip (not big knee-bend kicks → drag). Because most swimmers kick slower than they swim, take the *minimum* kicks to the breakout — typically **2 off a turn, 3 off a start** [source: https://theraceclub.com/aqua_note/freestyle-flip-turn-streamline-dolphin-kick-dolphin-kick-technique-number-of-kicks/].
- **Breakout:** start the first pull just before the head surfaces; don't stall the transition.
- Realistic adaptation after 4–6 weeks of focused work: breakout distance **+0.5–1m**, turn splits **−0.1–0.2s** [source: https://athletesuntapped.com/blog/the-fifth-stroke-mastering-underwater-phase-optimization-in-swimming/].
- **⚠️ Julian-specific:** dolphin-kick activation triggered his quad cramp. Until dryland builds quad/hip-flexor base, prescribe **push-and-glide off the wall, no dolphin kick**. His measured first-length-after-wall gap (L1 ~22.3s vs L2 ~19.7s) confirms push-off is his single biggest technical gap — so this matters, but must be developed via controlled loading, not ballistic work yet.

## 6. Periodization & progression

- Traditional model: **general → specific → competitive** phases over a 16–20 week macrocycle [source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sports-and-active-living/articles/10.3389/fspor.2025.1642020/full]. This maps onto Julian's Phase 1 (Sprint Development) → Phase 2 (Speed Integration) → Phase 3 (Race Sharpening).
- Early phases emphasise strength-endurance and coordination; the competitive phase shifts to **speed-strength and sprint capacity with reduced volume, higher intensity** [source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sports-and-active-living/articles/10.3389/fspor.2025.1642020/full].
- **Progression within a phase:** tighten one variable at a time per session (e.g. sprint target −0.3s, threshold pace −3s/100m, SWOLF −1) off the rolling best. Don't compound multiple step-downs at once.
- **Taper (Phase 3):** 1–3 weeks; **sprinters need a shorter taper**. Reduce volume **50–80%** while holding intensity; expect to peak **2–4 weeks** after taper onset [source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3873657/] [source: https://www.bretthawke.com/blog/guide-to-tapering-for-sprint-swimming]. Keep race-pace speed touches; cut the volume, not the sharpness.

### Phase-by-phase detail + advancement

Advancement is **block-based**, not calendar weeks: each phase runs for a fixed number of completed blocks (a block = 3 pool + 1 dryland). The per-phase performance numbers below are **progress targets, not pass/fail gates** — the phase rolls over on its block quota regardless of whether the targets are hit. Targets are tracked/displayed to show how progress is tracking.

The targets step **monotonically** from the current state (25m ~16.6s, 50m ~34.3s, avg SWOLF ~27) toward the sub-30 goal — one stretch per phase off the rolling best, with the phase's *intent* deciding which metric leads (P1 = top-end speed, so 25m leads; P2 = race-pace integration, so 50m leads; P3 = taper/peak to the goal). Within a phase the 25m target runs ahead of the 50m on purpose: raw speed is built first, then sustained over the full distance.

**Phase 1 — Sprint Development** (6 blocks; pool priority: Sprint > Technique > Threshold; sprint finish in ALL subtypes)
- Goal: break the long SWOLF plateau, build neuromuscular top-end speed.
- Signature work: 8–16×25 max @ ≥2 min rest, priming builds, push-off glide drills, threshold maintenance.
- Progress targets: best 25m → 15.5s, avg SWOLF → 27, 50m → 33.0s.

**Phase 2 — Speed Integration** (6 blocks; pool priority: Race-Pace > Sprint > Technique; includes sprint finish)
- Goal: integrate that speed into the 50 — lactate tolerance + holding race pace under fatigue; sharpen turns/underwaters.
- Signature work: race-pace 50s (the most practical race-tolerance rep), 4×50 fast @ ~1:4 work:rest with rest shrinking as you adapt, broken 50s (25+25), short-rest 20×25 lactate sets, maintained sprint speed [source: https://oswestryotters.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/Lactate-Tolerance.pdf] [source: https://www.traindaly.com/train-daly/blog/master-your-race-pace-how-to-find-your-ideal-swimming-speed].
- Progress targets: best 25m → 14.5s, avg SWOLF → 25, race-pace 50m → 31.0s.

**Phase 3 — Race Sharpening** (4 blocks, then terminal; pool priority: Race Simulation > Sprint > Recovery; **no** mandatory sprint-finish block — every fast swim is the point)
- Goal: taper and peak for the sub-30 attempt. Shorter warm-ups (200–300m), sharply reduced volume, maintained intensity [source: https://www.bretthawke.com/blog/guide-to-tapering-for-sprint-swimming].
- Signature work: timed race-simulation 50s from a push with full recovery, breakout/bodyline speed. Readiness cues: crisp warm-ups, fast times without forcing, confidence.
- Progress targets: 50m → sub-30.0s, best 25m → 14.0s, avg SWOLF → 23. Terminal phase — the sub-30 50m attempt; no auto-advance.

## 7. Dryland for sprint freestyle

- Build on the **stability → strength → power** continuum [source: https://www.yourswimlog.com/sprint-freestyle-dryland/]. Don't add plyometrics before joint stability and base strength exist.
- **Pulling (lat-dominant):** pull-ups (gold standard — mimic the freestyle pull), inverted/ring rows, lat work [source: https://swimlikeafish.org/6-dryland-exercises-for-a-stronger-freestyle-pull].
- **Core (anti-rotation + rotation):** planks, hollow-body, L-sit progressions, V-ups — water punishes a weak trunk; train *stopping* and *starting* rotation.
- **Shoulder stability:** scapular control, prone Y-T-W, external rotation — injury prevention for a pull-heavy program. (Lat pullover is **permanently retired** for Julian — caused shoulder discomfort.)
- **Controlled quad/hip-flexor loading (Julian priority):** bar-assisted deep-squat holds, single-leg squats, slow standing hip-flexor drives, glute bridges, single-leg deadlifts — build the base **before** any explosive push-off/plyometric work [source: https://www.yourswimlog.com/prevent-muscle-cramps-swimming/].
- Equipment redundancy: programmable for **rings only** or a **calisthenic park (bars)** — both can deliver pulling, core, shoulder, and controlled-leg work.

## 8. Injury & cramp management (Julian-critical)

- **Causes of quad/hip-flexor cramp in swimmers:** dehydration, fatigue, electrolyte deficit, weak/tight hip flexors, over-pointed-toe tension, and **ramping intensity too fast** [source: https://www.a3performance.com/blogs/a3-performance/swimming-muscle-cramps-types-causes-prevention-and-treatment] [source: https://www.yourswimlog.com/prevent-muscle-cramps-swimming/].
- **Prevention:** hydrate; **build intensity gradually**; keep feet/ankles relaxed; strengthen glutes/hamstrings/hip flexors (glute bridges, single-leg deadlifts, core/pilates-based work).
- **Training modifications for active flag:** no dolphin kick / explosive push-off; push-and-glide wall drills only; keep ≥2 min rest between max efforts (the protective variable that prevented recurrence); keep isometric hip-flexor holds (L-sit) short and progress slowly; stop the set on any tightness rather than pushing through.

## 9. Testing & benchmarks

- **50m race segmentation:** Start & Breakout (0–15m), Speed Carry (15–25m), Maintenance (25–40m), Final Sprint (40–50m). Velocity drops from >3 m/s (0–15m) → ~2.4 m/s (15–25m) → ~2.2 m/s (25–35m) — segmenting pinpoints weak spots [source: https://www.accio.com/sportshealth/how-to-swim-50m-freestyle-under-25-seconds-the-start-underwater-phase-focus].
- **Testing without live pace (Forerunner 255):** time everything manually/by interval; use **broken swims** (e.g. 50 as 25+25 with 10s rest) to hold race-pace speed and read the gap to a continuous 50; periodic **50m time-trial from a push, full recovery** as the headline benchmark.
- **25m-pool advantage:** a tight, explosive push-off carries speed into the final 15m — the wall is a free speed source in short course [source: https://swimmingregimen.com/blog/50m-swim-time-standards].
- **Benchmark context:** competitive adult male 50m LCM ~28–33s; Julian's ~38s equivalent → sub-30 is a realistic multi-block target via the DPS + push-off + CO2-tolerance levers above [source: https://swimmingregimen.com/blog/50m-swim-time-standards].
- **Use SWOLF as the efficiency tracker** between time-trials.

## Deterministic markers derived from this research

These are computable from Garmin data (per-length time, SWOLF, stroke count, HR) and are wired into the engine (`garmin-parser.js`, `flags.js`). They make the tracker more adaptive without an LLM:

| Marker | Definition | Why it matters | Lives in |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Distance per stroke (DPS)** | pool_length ÷ strokes/length | Strongest 50m speed predictor; the efficiency lever | parser summary + per length |
| **Stroke rate (SPM)** | strokes ÷ length_time × 60 | The other half of velocity; watch for rate without DPS | parser summary + per length |
| **Sprint consistency** | spread (max−min) of sprint-rep times | Repeatable speed = trained speed; high spread = pacing/fatigue issue | flags |
| **Velocity fade** | last sprint rep − first sprint rep time | Fade across a max set flags fatigue / inadequate rest | flags |
| **Sprint rest adherence** | rest taken after each sprint rep ≥120s? | The alactic-quality AND quad-protective variable | parser (rest capture) + flags |
